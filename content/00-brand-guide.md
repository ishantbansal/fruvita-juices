## Brand Positioning: Natural & Wholesome
Fruvita is a real-fruit juice brand built on the idea of honest, farm-close refreshment — juice that tastes like the fruit it came from, not like a lab. Warm, trustworthy, a little nostalgic (think orchards, monsoon markets, family kitchens) without being twee.

### Brand Pillars
1. **Real fruit, real taste** — every bottle should read as "made from actual fruit," not synthetic flavoring.
2. **No nasties** — no artificial colors, no preservatives (state only if true for your actual formulation — verify before printing on packaging, this is a regulatory claim).
3. **Rooted, not generic** — flavors like Matka Jeera lean into regional/local flavor heritage rather than generic "tropical punch" branding.
4. **Everyday indulgence** — positioned as a daily refreshment, not an occasional treat; affordable enough for regular purchase.

### Tone of Voice
- Warm, plainspoken, a little playful — never corporate or clinical.
- Short sentences. Sensory language (juicy, sun-ripened, chilled, bursting) over technical language.
- Avoid superlative overload ("world's best," "#1") — let specific detail (real % fruit content, no added color) do the persuading.
- Hinglish-friendly touches are fine for India-market flavors (e.g., Matka Jeera) — don't over-translate cultural flavors into generic English.

### Tagline Options (pick one, or A/B test)
1. **"Real Fruit. Real Fruvita."**
2. **"Straight From the Orchard, Into the Bottle."**
3. **"Juice, the Way Fruit Intended."**
4. **"Every Sip, Sun-Ripened."**
5. **"Fruvita — Nothing Added, Everything Real."** (only use if formulation genuinely has zero additives — legal claim)

### Color Palette Direction (for design phase)
- Primary: warm mango-orange (#F5871F range) and deep guava-pink (#E4436D range) as accents
- Base: cream/off-white background (#FFF8EF range) for a natural, unbleached-paper feel — avoid stark white
- Accent green (#3A6B35 range) for leaf/orchard motifs
- Avoid neon/candy colors except for the "Thrill"/"Kiss"/"Electric" sub-line, which can go bolder to signal a fun/energetic variant

### Typography Direction
- Headline: a rounded, friendly serif or soft slab-serif (evokes hand-crafted, not corporate)
- Body: clean humanist sans-serif for legibility on packaging and web
